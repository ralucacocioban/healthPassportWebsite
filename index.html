HEALTHPASSPORT

“HealthPassport” is a complex system developed for people with learning disabilities and dementia that aims to revolutionise the way these people access technology and to help NHS develop a more digitalized way of collecting, displaying and analyzing patients’ data. This product has many functionalities: supporting their daily activities, making healthcare data more accessible to doctors, relatives and friends and bringing innovation in the way these people interact with and perceive technology.

  • Tell Me More

We are changing the way people with learning disabilities and dementia access technology

Our target group of users usually experience difficulties in: reading, taking care of themselves, understanding new or complex information, learning new skills and coping independently with mentally intensive tasks.
For these reasons, Health Passport is designed to support them in their daily activities and bring technology closer to them. The functionalities that it offers allow patients to store their personal details, objects they like/dislike, their allergies, emotions and encourages the users to take pictures through which they can express themselves or the way they feel.

Main features of HealthPassport

Digitalized Healthcare data

“HealthPassport” allows users to store their healthcare information such as allergies, level of understanding, type of communication prefered, number of support hours needed per day and other useful details. All this data is displayed on the website or within the “HealthPassport” app making all this information easily accessible and allowing patients to present their healthcare data without the need of carrying physical documents.

Option to take photos

Users of this system will be able to take photos and capture important moments in their life. By taking advantage of this feature, they will be able to interact with the app more often and will have the chance to express themselves and store photos of people, objects or events that impressed them.

Customizable contact list

“HealthPassport” allows its users to create their own list of important people in their life and customize this list by specifying the relationship with the person, providing a brief description etc.

Likes and Dislikes questionnaire

Users can easily express their preferences by liking or disliking various pictures. This data becomes very useful to NHS which uses a like/dislike technique in order to analyze and monitor the progress of their patients’ with learning disabilities and dementia.

Mood Expression

The “HealthPassport” product includes the possibility of expressing emotions such as happy, sad and in pain together with the chance to give reasons of what generated that specific mood. Our target audience will benefit from this since studies show that these people tend to be very introvert and can’t express their feelings publicly.

Events and Notifications

The system allows patients to add new events to the calendar specifying at the same time the type of event (birthday, appointment, medicine reminder). This functionality has a tremendous impact on people with learning disabilities and dementia since it uses a notification system to remind them about the upcoming events.

Availability

The system is designed to provide all the functionalities both online and offline. Whenever there is no connection to the Internet all the changes are stored locally and as soon as there is a contact with the network, any changes made to the local database are synchronised with the database stored on the server.

Extensibility

The system supports loose coupling and therefore it has potential to allow and accept extension of capabilities. Every single feature of the application can be easily reused without the need of rewriting the existing code. There is also an extensive system and user manual, which explains how to operate with the current API and lets developers take advantage of all the functionalities that we have implemented.

Cloud for pictures, events and contacts

Our system allows patients to store their pictures, events and contacts on the cloud. In this way, their data will always be securely saved and will be available to their doctors and relatives as well. Patients also have the opportunity to update or delete their information online so that they can keep their details up-to-date and can take advantage of synchronizing it with all their devices.

Encrypted passwords over https

The system is protected by an authorization system. We encrypt passwords and send them using https requests; therefore, we never store plain passwords. The system is not open to any of the security threats, such as SQL injection attack. The system supports Role-based access control (RBAC) model , which restricts system access to authorized users only.

Main features of DOCTOR'S DASHBOARD

Clinician's details

Members of the medical personnel are able to see their details: name, surname, email and telephone. They are also presented with direct links to the Patients tab, where they can see all people with learning disabilities or dementia, the Log Out functionality and the HealthPassport, where they can use the application as a patient and check the features it provides to them.

Patient's HealthPassport

Once people from the medical staff have selected a particular patient, this will be the first tab out of three that will be displayed in front of them. It contains all the personal details about the patient: name, surname, email, telephone, disability level, understanding level, communication type and support hours.

Patient's Likes/Dislikes

When doctors have navigated to this tab, they will be able to see the pictures that this particular person with learning disabilities or dementia has liked and disliked as well as all the possible images they can choose from. They are also able to upload new pictures towards which this person will have to express their preference.

Patient's Emotions Table

The emotions table provides information about a particular patient’s mood expressions that have been captured through the app. It describes several properties of each emotion including: emotion type, picture, location and date.

Emotions Graph

Once the people from the medical staff have reached this page, they will see a graph displayed in front of them. It represents the different mood expressions of the person with learning disabilities or dementia. When they hover over a particular point on the graph, they are able to see the type of emotion, its location and the date when it was indicated. There is also a range controller that they can use to select the period during which they are interested in seeing how this particular patient felt.

Emotions Map

Based on the location of each emotion expressed by the specific patient the map is populated with these coordinates. Thus, doctors are able to gain information about the whereabouts of the person with learning disabilities or dementia when they indicated how they felt.

Main features of THE SMARTWATCH EXTENSIONS

Emotions Extension

The aim of this extension is to encourage patients with learning disabilities to express their emotions through the use of a simple and intuitive app. It involves a friendly and easy to understand UI which contains three suggestive buttons that represent happy, sad and feeling pain. At the same time, the extension communicates with the phone and uses the phone’s GPS system in order to retrieve the location of users whenever they press a button on the smartwatch.

EVENTS EXTENSION

This extension is responsible for sending notifications to the smartwatch and inform patients about the upcoming events stored in the calendar. The functionality is designed to help patients remember mainly about taking their medications and attending their appointments.
